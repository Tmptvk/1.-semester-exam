Moving art

As the first big assignment involving the Adobe programs, we were asked to make a “Movingart” animation.
Movingart is a style of animation, often involving a classic painted, and then adding life to it via animation.
To start of with, we had to pick a painting to work with. I decided to pick “Banksy, Girl with Balloon” as my
paining, as I had already developed an idea for it. The original painting shows a little girl, who has just lost
her balloon. The way I decided to give life to this paining, was to make the balloon fly away from her and out
of the screen. In the workshop we used the program “animate” and to fully develop my animation I used two
tools namely the classic tween and the motion tween.

Before the workshop began, I researched the 12 principles of animation (The illusion of life, the 12 principles)
My animation follows the principle "Secondary action"
Secondary is when a secondary object or person, supports the main action in the animation
In this case, the balloon which is flying away from the girl, has a string attached to it
which waves in the wind, supporting the illusion of movement of the balloon
