Animation via CSS

This assignment was a workshop in Animation with CSS. We were all asked to make an animation using CSS
with a minimum of 12 frames. We could choose one of the follow to animate – A logo, a portrait or a mascot.
I decided to go with a mascot, as I came up with an idea pretty fast. I wanted to animate a dog sitting down
whacking it’s tail. I started of by doing a quick sketch of how I wanted my dog to look, so I knew what I wanted
when I made it digitally. To make the animation, I used Illustrator and made a sprite sheet. The dog itself is
made fully of anchor points and vectors, so it’s scalable no matter what size would be needed. The different
parts of the dog itself, was made in different layers to make it easier to move certain limbs in the frames.

After the sprites were made, I then needed to actually animate the figure. A limit was then found to the
animation I wanted to make. I originally wanted to make 60 frames in total, but ended up having to settle
for 42. Illustrator has a limit to how wide a spreadsheet can be, which limited me to 40 frames of 400x400 pixels.
The only way to get around this problem, would be to learn Javascript. With an approaching deadline, I had
to settle for 42 frames in total. In the code itself, I decided to make it repeat itself an infinite number of
times, to make the animation more smooth.
